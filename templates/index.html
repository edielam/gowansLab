About Us

Our Lab is interested in investigating the Genetic Architecture of Dental, Oral and Craniofacial Phenotypes

Craniofacial malformations that result from abnormal development comprise over one third of all congenital birth defects . High-throughput sequencing technologies have enhanced the identification of many causative disease genes, with functional studies helping to clarify the mechanisms of action of these genes.

These findings in craniofacial genetics,are largely reported in populations of Caucasian ancestry with populations of African ancestry poorly represented. The aim of this lab is to expand the scope of this study by investigating populations of African ancestry, with the hope of increasing the understanding of normal craniofacial development by carrying out human genetic studies, as well as developmental studies in animal models and cell lines.
